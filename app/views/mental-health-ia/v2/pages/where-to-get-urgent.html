Get urgent help now for mental health

Where to get urgent help for mental health

If you need urgent help for a mental health crisis, emergency or breakdown, it's important to know that support is available.

NHS mental health crisis support

The NHS provides 24-hour mental health crisis services.

These services can:

  • help you speak to a qualified mental health professional
  • access your patient history, if needed
  • arrange further support, if needed - such as an urgent face-to-face-assessment

If you've already been given a crisis line number to use in an emergency, it's best to call it.

If you just need to talk, any time of day or night

Free listening services

These services offer confidential advice from trained volunteers. You can talk about anything thats troubling you, no matter how difficult:

  • Call: 116 123 or talk to Samaritans
  • Text "SHOUT" to 85258 to contact Shout Crisis Text Line

If you're under 19:

  • Call: 0800 1111 to talk to Childline
  • Text "YM" to 85258 to contact Shout Crisis Text Line

You can also email jo@samaritans.org or visit a Samaritans branch to talk to someone.

Information:

Coping during a crisis

The mental health charity Mind have information on ways to help yourself cope during a crisis

This includes calming exercises and a tool to get you through the next few hours.

Urgent advice: Get advice from 111 or ask for an urgent GP appointment if:

  • you are not able to speak to a local crisis support service
  • you need help urgently for your mental health, but it's not an emergency
  • you're not sure what to do

You may be able to speak to a nurse or mental health nurse over the phone.

Go to 111.nhs.uk or call: 111.

A GP can help you access mental health services. You may also be able to refer yourself to some services.

Immediate action required: Call 999 or go to A&E now if:

  • someone's life is at risk - for example they have seriously injured themselves or taken an overdose
  • you don't feel you can keep yourself or someone else safe

A mental health emergency should be taken as seriously as a physical one. You will not be wasting anyone's time.

Call: 999

Find your nearest A&E

How a mental health emergency is treated in A&E

If you go to A&E they should treat you with respect and look after any immediate physical and mental health needs.

They should also refer you to a liaison psychiatry service or local crisis resolution and home treatment team (CRHT).

You can read more about

  • liaison psychiatry services on the Royal College of Psychiatrists website
  • crisis resolution and home treatment teams (CRHTs) on the Mind website

The team in charge of your care will assess you and decide on the best course of care.

They may admit you to hospital or decide you can go home.

Making a safety plan

If you struggle with suicidal thoughts or are supporting someone else, it may help to make a safety plan to use if you need it:

  • the Staying Safe website provides information on how to make a safety plan, including video tutorials and online templates to guide you through the process
  • the mental health charity Mind also provide information on planning for a mental health crisis
