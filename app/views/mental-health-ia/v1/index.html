How might we questions

  • How might we support users who need to quickly find urgent MH help?
  • How might we consider people's feelings and symptoms?
  • How might we make our navigation more obvious?
  • How might we signpost content for diagnosed conditions vs. symptoms?
  • How might we encourage information seeking behaviour?
  • How might we surface common tasks?

Design challenges

  • Making urgent help prominent
  • Groupings that make sense but also don't overwhelm Level 0
  • Language that isn't too strict but still sounds like the NHS
  • Stressing importance of MH symptoms just as much as physical health

Assumptions

  • We believe putting the get help now link on every mental health page will make users feel that we are taking mental health seriously because help is available on every page quickly, and we will see users either click on this or remark that they find it useful.
  • We believe that the order on Level 0 is the most helpful for users because it is ordered in a way of most visited, and we will see users be able to find what they need quickly.
  • We believe that users who are undiagnosed will be able to find information to help them because we have separated symptoms and feelings from conditions, and we will see users from this group tell us this is useful.
  • We believe that using common names like 'depression' and 'anxiety' rather than the longer clinical names will be more helpful for people and we will see users know what to find when they click on these.
  • We believe that people will be able to navigate in and out of the topic hubs because they are visually different to level 1 and other level 2 hubs and we will see users navigate between these and understand the difference.
